## Root cause recap

The old SectionState::TryAlloc held the section mutex across the synchronous
~300ms NRW write to StoreSvr. Because the section mutex is a plain std::mutex,
brpc bthreads blocking on it do not yield their pthread workers. With all 8
benchmark threads hitting one section, all 8 brpc pthreads were occupied
(1 doing the fetch join, 7 blocked on the mutex), so brpc could not schedule
the bthreads needed to receive StoreSvr RPC responses — the NRW write itself
timed out (confirmations=0/3) and client RPCs piled up until the 500ms client
timeout, producing cascading STORE_ERROR.

The fix moves the StoreSvr write to a dedicated background thread per section
that runs outside the section lock and outside any brpc bthread, with a 50%
watermark prefetch so the buffer is normally refilled before exhaustion.
